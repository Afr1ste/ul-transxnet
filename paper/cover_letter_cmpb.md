Dear Editor,

We would like to submit our manuscript entitled "Localization-Robust Teacher-Student Learning for Mobile Ultrasound Lesion Classification" for consideration as an Original Research article in Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine.

The submission is appropriate for Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine because it focuses on formal computing methods for biomedical image analysis and their practical implementation in medical imaging workflows. Ultrasound lesion classification is clinically relevant but technically sensitive to lesion localization, acquisition variability, calibration, and edge-device deployment. Existing studies often report strong oracle-ROI or single-dataset results without fully separating localization assumptions, dataset-specific evaluation, mobile latency, and true cross-organ transfer.

Our study adds a localization-stress teacher-student workflow that links these components in one fixed reporting protocol. We first analyze TransXNet-family teachers with MCA/MUDD/differential-attention ablation, then use the same GT/noisy/detector/full-image ROI protocol to verify whether EfficientFormer students preserve localization robustness after distillation. We finally measure exported students on two Android devices and explicitly separate joint multi-organ training from leave-one-domain-out transfer. The resulting evidence supports robustness-aware mobile feasibility while avoiding unsupported claims of clinical deployment readiness or universal cross-organ generalization.

We confirm that this manuscript has not been published previously and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine. The datasets used in this study are publicly available, de-identified ultrasound datasets; no new patient data were collected by the authors.
